The Tower in the City: Český Yacht Klub Guesthouse

Location: Prague, Czech Republic
Year: 2018 - 2019
Project objective: Study Project

The birthplace of Czech sailing is located in an old neighborhood of Prague called Podoli, 3km south of the city center. Here stands the venerable clubhouse of the Czech Yacht Club on the dam of the harbor. The house, built entirely of wood in 1912, has been continuously expanded and renovated over the course of more than 100 years but has managed to retain its charm.

By now, it seems that the expansion potential of the yacht club has been exhausted.

A campanile is a freestanding bell tower next to a church without a direct connection.

Just as a campanile extends a church without being directly connected to it, my design aims to expand the yacht club without direct attachment.

The existing building faces the harbor side, so I place my tower on the opposite side of the dam. This way, each building has its own side, and my tower does not overshadow the old yacht club but rather extends the club's presence toward the river.

To emphasize the unity of the two buildings, I adopt the wooden construction method. However, the vertical load-bearing in my tower is achieved through external steel supports, and the facade is more open.

On the ground floor, a pier-like wooden deck invites visitors to step closer to the waterfront to participate more directly in the activities on the river.

The tower is intended as a guesthouse for the yacht club.
